Chapter 1 Utility

Utility and diminishing marginal utility

Utility is a measure of the level of hapiness or satisfaction that someone receives from the consumption of a good.

Marginal utility is the additional utility gained when consuming one more unit of a good

The law of diminishing marginal utility sugguests that as consumption of a good increases, the marginal utility decreases.

→ For example, suppose you were hungry and wanted a slice of pizza. Lets say you would pay $2 for the first slice. If you wanted a second slice, you might be willing to pay less, perhaps $1.50. If you wanted a third slice, you might be willing to pay even less, perhaps $1.25. This is because the utility or satisfaction you derived from consuming an additional slice of pizza has decreased as you eat more.

The equi-marginal principle

A consumer is said to be in consumer equilibrium when it is not possible to increase total utility by reallocating expenditure between any of the goods availible, for example from good A to good B. This is reffered to as the equi-marginal principle, where a consumer has allocated income in a way that has maximised utility.

The equi-marginal principle is based on the following assumptions:

  1. Consumers have limited incomes
  2. Consumers will always behave in a rational manner
  3. Consumers seek to maximise their utility

Limitations of marginal utitlity theory and assumptions of rational behavior

Marginal Utility theory assumes that consumers are capable of putting their wants in rank order and a value to satisfaction gained from consumption. However, this is not always the case. The law of diminishing marginal utility further assumes that consumers act in a rational way in their purchasing decisions, which is another major assumption. Real-world evidence shows that there are various other factors apart from utility that determine what we purchase.

Key Terms

Utility: the satisfaction received from consumption.

Marginal Utility: the utility derived from the consumption of one more unit of the good or service.

Law of Diminishing Marginal Utility: as consumption increases, the satisfaction from consumption decreases.

Equi-marginal Principle: consumers maximise their utility where their marginal valuation for each product consumed is the same
